# U.S. Macro Liquidity and Fed Policy Regime Guide

Use this reference when analyzing U.S. liquidity, Federal Reserve policy direction, rate-cut or rate-hike probabilities, Treasury issuance, term premium, money-market plumbing, reserve scarcity, recession versus inflation, or macro transmission into equities, bonds, gold, USD, and high-duration growth assets.

Core principle: do not ask "hawkish or dovish?" first. Classify the macro state first, then infer the Fed reaction function and market playbook. One payroll print, one CPI print, or one speech is not enough; require alignment across inflation/labor, fiscal/term-premium, liquidity plumbing, real-demand support, and market transmission.

## Method Quality

This framework is usable and economically grounded if applied as a probabilistic state machine:

- It is scientific when each scenario is tied to observable indicators, direction of change, and a confirmation window.
- It is weak when labels such as "hawk", "dove", "recession", or "liquidity crisis" are assigned from a single headline.
- It is dangerous when "employment down + inflation down = immediate rate cuts" is treated as automatic; financial conditions, market functioning, fiscal supply, and inflation expectations can override that simple rule.

Use official or primary data when possible: BLS for labor/inflation/ECI, BEA for PCE, Treasury QRA and auction data for issuance, New York Fed/FRED for SOFR, IORB, ON RRP, reserves, SRF/repo operations, and New York Fed ACM term-premium data.

## Five Daily Variable Groups

Evaluate these five groups together. Do not let one group dominate unless it is a plumbing stress event.

| Group | What to watch | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Inflation and labor second derivative | Core services ex-housing / supercore proxy, ECI, average hourly earnings, payroll breadth, unemployment trend, claims, JOLTS, energy impulse | Determines whether the Fed can focus on growth weakness or must keep inflation credibility first. |
| Fiscal issuance and term premium | Treasury QRA, bill versus coupon mix, auction tails/bid-to-cover/dealer takedown, TGA path, deficit surprises, ACM/market term premium | Separates monetary policy rate expectations from duration-supply and risk-premium shocks. |
| Liquidity plumbing | SOFR-IORB, EFFR-IORB, repo rates, ON RRP, reserve balances, TGA, SRF/repo take-up, quarter-end/calendar effects | Identifies reserve scarcity or money-market stress before it appears in risk assets. |

## Scenario State Machine

Assign probabilities across states; do not force a single answer.

### 1. Baseline Bear Steepening

Features:

- Growth resilient, inflation sticky or re-accelerating at the margin.
- Long-end yields rise faster than front-end yields.
- Term premium or real yields rise; equities can bifurcate between AI/growth leaders and rate-sensitive laggards.
- Volatility rises but funding markets remain functional.

Confirm with:

- 10y/30y yields rising with stable or less dovish policy-rate expectations.
- Term-premium estimates rising.
- Payroll breadth and ECI/wages still resilient.
- No persistent SOFR-IORB or repo stress.

Asset read:

- Negative for long-duration Treasuries and rate-sensitive defensives.
- Mixed for equities; AI/capex winners can keep working if earnings revisions are strong.
- USD and real yields tend to stay supported.

### 2. Inflation Bear Flattening

Features:

- Inflation acceleration dominates growth weakness.
- Front-end and short-dated real rates reprice higher; rate-cut window closes.
- Market shifts from "soft landing" to "Fed forced to stay tight or hike".

Confirm with:

- Core services/supercore and ECI/wages accelerate together.
- Breakevens and inflation swaps rise while front-end rates sell off.
- Fed communication pushes against easing.
- Labor slack is insufficient to offset inflation pressure.

Asset read:

- Broadly negative for equities, especially high-duration multiple expansion.
- USD and cash-like yields stay strong.
- Gold response is ambiguous: negative from real yields, supported only if credibility/fiscal stress rises.

### 3. Fiscal Issuance / Term-Premium Shock

Features:

- Treasury supply, deficit concerns, or auction weakness pushes long yields higher independent of the expected policy path.
- Long-end selloff is supply/risk-premium driven rather than pure inflation.

Confirm with:

- QRA or auction data imply heavier coupon supply or poor absorption.
- Auction tails widen, dealer takedown rises, indirect demand weakens.
- ACM or market-implied term premium rises.
- Long-end yields rise while Fed cut expectations do not move equivalently.

Asset read:

- Negative for duration and valuation-sensitive assets.
- Can pressure fiscal-sensitive assets and lift USD if global demand for dollars remains strong.
- Do not call this "hawkish Fed" unless front-end policy expectations also reprice.

### 4. Liquidity Plumbing Tightening

Features:

- Money-market rates show reserve scarcity or distribution stress.
- Plumbing stress can dominate inflation and growth narratives in the short run.

Confirm with:

- SOFR-IORB or EFFR-IORB rises persistently toward zero or positive territory, especially outside normal quarter-end noise.
- Repo rates spike relative to administered rates.
- ON RRP buffer is low or falling while TGA rebuild drains reserves.
- Reserve balances decline and SRF/repo usage appears or grows.
- Funding pressure transmits into Treasury volatility and risk assets.

Asset read:

- First response may be risk-off and USD-positive.
- If the Fed uses technical tools such as repo operations, SRF, reserve-management adjustments, or QT changes, distinguish plumbing repair from a true dovish pivot.
- In this state, do not overinterpret speeches; the plumbing data lead.

### 5. Traditional Recession / Disinflation

Features:

- Labor weakens broadly and inflation momentum falls together.
- Fed reaction can return to the rate-cut path, but only after inflation credibility is not threatened.

Confirm with:

- Payroll breadth deteriorates, unemployment and claims trend higher, hours and income weaken.
- Core inflation and wage momentum decelerate.
- Credit spreads widen and earnings breadth weakens.
- Real yields fall because growth deteriorates, not because inflation fears rise.

Asset read:

- Treasuries rally if inflation falls enough.
- Equities may fall first on earnings risk, then recover when liquidity easing dominates.
- Gold tends to improve when real yields fall and the Fed pivot becomes credible.

## Four Playbooks

Use these playbooks after scoring the five variable groups.

### Baseline: A > C > B

Meaning: inflation/labor and real-demand support dominate; fiscal supply matters; plumbing stress is manageable.

Typical signs:

- Inflation is high/sticky, employment is stable, fiscal supply is large, AI/capex remains strong.
- Higher volatility, bear steepening, and fewer credible rate-cut promises.

Trading posture:

- Prefer assets with earnings/cash-flow support over pure duration.
- Be cautious with long bonds unless term premium stabilizes.

### Edge Decoupling: A > B > C

Meaning: supply shock or inflation impulse enters wages/expectations; the rate-cut window closes.

Typical signs:

- Energy/import/commodity pressure feeds inflation expectations and wage bargaining.
- Front-end rates turn more restrictive; "short-end re-hardening" appears.

Trading posture:

- Reduce high-duration growth exposure unless earnings revisions are exceptional.
- USD and cash yields remain attractive.

### Plumbing Failure: A Is Overridden

Meaning: funding-market stress overrides the normal inflation/recession debate.

Typical signs:

- SOFR-IORB or repo stress rises, reserves/TGA/ON RRP configuration tightens, SRF/repo usage appears.
- Treasury market liquidity deteriorates.

Trading posture:

- Focus on central-bank plumbing tools rather than hawk/dove rhetoric.
- Distinguish liquidity repair from a macro easing cycle.

### Traditional Recession

Meaning: employment breaks and inflation falls; policy can return to easing.

Typical signs:

- Job-market deterioration becomes broad, inflation momentum rolls over, credit spreads widen.

Trading posture:

- Long-duration bonds can work after inflation confirmation.
- Equities may need an earnings reset before policy easing helps.

## Scoring Template

Score each group from -2 to +2:

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| +2 | Strongly inflationary, restrictive, or risk-tightening impulse. |
| +1 | Mildly inflationary/restrictive/tightening. |
| 0 | Mixed or neutral. |
| -1 | Mildly disinflationary/easing/liquidity-supportive. |
| -2 | Strongly recessionary/disinflationary/easing or liquidity-relief impulse. |

Suggested weights:

| Group | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Inflation and labor second derivative | 30 |
| Fiscal issuance and term premium | 20 |
| Liquidity plumbing | 25 |
| AI/capex and real-demand support | 10 |
| Market transmission | 15 |

Interpretation:

- Positive aggregate score: restrictive/liquidity-tightening macro pressure.
- Negative aggregate score: easing/disinflationary pressure.
- High plumbing score (+2) can override the aggregate for short-horizon risk management.
- Divergent scores imply a mixed regime; present scenario probabilities instead of a single call.

## Indicator Rules of Thumb

Use rules of thumb as warnings, not hard laws:

- SOFR-IORB: rising toward zero or positive for several sessions is a reserve-distribution warning; adjust for quarter-end and Treasury settlement dates.
- ON RRP: decline is not automatically bearish if reserves remain ample; it becomes dangerous when ON RRP is low, TGA rises, and reserves fall together.
- TGA: rebuild drains reserves; drawdown adds reserves. Always combine TGA with RRP and reserve balances.
- Term premium: rising term premium with stable front-end expectations is a fiscal/supply shock; rising front-end expectations is a policy shock.
- ECI/wages: wage acceleration with sticky services inflation is more Fed-relevant than one noisy payroll number.
- Payrolls: use breadth, hours, unemployment, claims, and revisions; do not rely only on headline nonfarm payrolls.
- AI/capex: strong AI/capex can keep equity leadership alive even under high rates, but it does not neutralize funding stress.
- Market transmission: MOVE leads rate-vol stress; VIX confirms equity stress; credit spreads confirm broad financial tightening.

## Output Format

When using this reference, include:

1. Data and horizon: latest data window and whether data are current, stale, or partial.
2. State probabilities: baseline bear steepening, inflation bear flattening, fiscal/term-premium shock, plumbing tightening, traditional recession/disinflation.
3. Five-group dashboard: score, evidence, and missing data for each group.
4. Fed reaction read: policy-rate path, balance-sheet/plumbing tools, and communication bias.
5. Asset transmission: equities, Treasuries, USD, gold, credit, and high-duration growth.
6. Invalidation triggers: what data would flip the state.

Never present the output as a deterministic Fed forecast. Always distinguish policy-rate decisions from plumbing operations, and distinguish liquidity repair from a true dovish pivot.
